⎰ A Bit of My Journey ⎱

My professional journey to-date has been fun and interesting overall. I studied English, Business, and Film at UCLA after dropping out of my Computer Science and Engineering major there since I was having a challenging time with my early CS class. I didn't have much of a strong CS background though I have always loved technical realms like video games, the web, etc. Ever since I was a kid, I wanted to make movies and so I stepped into my American Literature and Culture major and performed well there and truly loved it.

However, when I graduated from college, I had to get a job and I happened to 'long story short,' I fell into Finance and did surprisingly well. I traded Commodities at a Futures Commission Merchant near LA and grew my book of business. Still, I wasn't pleased with the overall philosophy of the company where I worked and I went to work for a few more Commodity brokerage firms in LA. None of these firms matched my overall philosophy so I moved to Oregon and left Finance as an employee, and strategized to launch my own Commodity brokerage firm and Commodity Trading Advisor.

I performed well, yet had some rocky market experiences here and there as well and I made some strategic business mistakes as well. Hence, I took a few leaves of absence to go after a few more entrepreneurial ventures, both in hardware and marketing, etc. What I learned was that I want to make sure that I am at the helm of a startup (as opposed to being second fiddle) and that currently I love building two businesses, one in private education called Learnhall and one in Commodity trading called LikeAHedgeFund. Both are businesses that can grow and scale with a founder who possesses exemplary technical skills. My last venture had a technical co-founder, yet I misread his ambition level. Hence, this is why I am here at LaunchCode. I also wish to have these tech skills so that I can be nimble in the future with regards to my professional career and goals as I might have other ventures that I wish to build down the road.

What I especially love about coding is it can help me as a tech entrepreneur. While I might be interested in the right opportunity as an employee, I mostly wish to learn coding so that I can seek to grow my startups and try to succeed well in the business world and have staying power in said world. I love learning and solving real problems in the business world and I believe that tech skills learned at LC will help me throughout the rest of my life.
